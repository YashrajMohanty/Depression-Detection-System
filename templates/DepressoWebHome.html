ML for Mental Health

Depression is a common and serious medical illness that negatively affects how you feel, the way you think and how you act. Depression causes feelings of sadness and/or a loss of interest in activities you once enjoyed.

It can lead to a variety of emotional and physical problems and can decrease your ability to function at work and at home. Other symptoms may include changes in appetite and weight, sleep disturbances, fatigue, difficulty concentrating, feelings of guilt or worthlessness and more.

Machine learning is a branch of artificial intelligence in computer science which makes use of data algorithms to emulate human learning, gradually bettering it's accuracy and efficiency. Machine learning has the potential to play a significant role in improving mental health care in several ways:

  • Early detection and diagnosis
  • Personalized treatment plans
  • Predictive analytics
  • Sentiment analysis
  • Virtual mental health assistants

To get a glimpse into what machine learning can do, you can try our depression prediction system here.

Depression affects an estimated one in 15 adults (6.7%) in any given year. And one in six people (16.6%) will experience depression at some time in their life. Depression can occur at any time, but on average, first appears during the late teens to mid-20s.

Being sad is not the same as having depression. Both grief and depression may involve intense sadness and withdrawal from usual activities. Grief and depression can co-exist. When grief and depression co-occur, the grief is more severe and lasts longer than grief without depression.

Fortunately, depression is a treatable condition. While it can be a challenging and sometimes chronic illness, the majority of people with depression(80-90%) can find relief and improve their quality of life with appropriate treatment.
